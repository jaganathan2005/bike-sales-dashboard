# 🚲 Bike Sales Dashboard

An interactive dashboard analyzing bike purchase behavior across 1,000 customers — built to identify who buys, who doesn't, and what patterns separate the two groups.

![Bike Sales Dashboard](![alt text](Bike_Dashboard.png))

## 📌 Overview

This dashboard breaks down bike purchase data by gender, education level, age group, commute distance, and region to uncover the customer profile most likely to convert. It's designed for quick, filter-driven exploration using interactive slicers.

## 🛠️ Tools Used

- Microsoft Excel (Pivot Tables, Pivot Charts, Slicers)
- KPI Cards for quick-glance metrics
- Data cleaning and formula-based calculated fields

## 🔍 Key Insights

- **Middle-aged customers show the strongest purchase intent** — this age bracket peaks well above Adolescent and Old groups in both "Yes" and "No" categories, making them the core target segment.
- **Bachelor's degree holders are the largest buying group** by volume, followed closely by Partial College — education level correlates with purchase likelihood.
- **Higher income is associated with higher purchase conversion** — both Female and Male buyers with "Yes" outcomes show higher average income than non-buyers.
- **Short commute customers (0-1 miles) dominate purchase volume** — proximity to work/commute distance appears to be a meaningful factor in bike adoption.
